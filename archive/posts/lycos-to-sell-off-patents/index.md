I read today the news that early Search Engine Lycos is selling off their patents, presumably to enable them to launch other services. This was stated in the betaboston article [Internet search pioneer Lycos puts patents up for sale](http://www.betaboston.com/news/2015/05/20/internet-search-pioneer-lycos-puts-patents-up-for-sale/).

I wondered how many patents Lycos might have, and what they covered, so I looked them up. Lycos hasn’t been around as a search engine for a few years, and yet there may be some value left in these patents. Some look very interesting, like the one about collections (Google just came out with [a couple of patents on collections](https://www.seobythesea.com/2015/05/how-google-is-focusing-upon-building-and-promoting-entity-collections/).)
