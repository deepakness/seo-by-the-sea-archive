Imagine being able to tap into an advertising network on the web that allowed you to upload your ads for display, where many people will see them offline. A new patent granted today describes a method for doing that using electronic billboards.

Imagine that you’re driving to work and pass an electronic billboard that would be an ideal place for an ad for your business. You notice that it’s not presently showing an ad but has a web address displayed, along with a message to “advertise here.” You repeat the address over and over a few times to try to remember it.

You get to your office, fire up a browser, and visit the URL that you’ve been chanting for a couple of minutes now.

The page shows the rates for advertising on that electronic billboard, some editorial guidelines, and a way to register and submit payment for showing an ad. Then, after registering and brainstorming for a few minutes on what you would like the billboard to say, you create an ad using PowerPoint, submit it, enter your credit card information, and set the time and duration to display it.

As you are waiting for editorial review, you notice that there are many other locations available, both indoors and outdoors, throughout the United States and in other countries, and you start looking at some of those. There’s also a section on how to broadcast messages wirelessly from each of the electronic billboards too.

You receive a notification that your electronic billboard is now live and leave the office to take a look while bringing your digital camera so that you have a picture of your first electronic billboard ad.

Here’s the patent:

[System and method for selling advertising space on electronic billboards over the internet](https://patents.google.com/patent/US7038637B1/en)
Inventors: Marc Eller and Zvi Yaniv
Assigned to SI Diamond Technology, Inc. (Austin, TX)
US Patent 7,038,637
Granted May 2, 2006
Filed April 20, 2000

Abstract


> Electronic billboards, which may be indoor or outdoor, are located in various geographical areas. Associated with each billboard is a web address. A client desiring to display information, such as an advertisement, on any selected electronic billboard can upload the information over the Internet to the server implementing the billboard website. The client can select the time and duration for the information to be displayed and can even purchase the display time using a credit card or some other type of account.

I’m not sure what the expense might be in setting an electronic billboard up, but the ease in changing ads to include very recent information might be something that makes up for it.
